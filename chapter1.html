1.3 Probability Theories

The term probability refers to an area or study which measures the chance of occurrence of a particular event.

It is possible to determine the chance of loss using probability theory. Provided that there is a large number of loss exposure.

This approach we may use to anticipate whether the risk/loss may happen or not. (usually used by insurance companies)

A Priori Probability

Determined when the total number of possible events are known are rare.

Example: The probability of getting a six on a roll of dice is 1/6.

This concept has a limited practical application in the study of risk because situations where a number of possible outcome are known are very rare.

Empirical Probability

Determined on the basis of historical data.

Example: Road accidents.

The underlying concept that makes it possible for empirical probability to be measured accurately is that as the number of observations increases, the predicted loss tends to approach the actual loss (the law of large numbers).

Judgmental Probability

Determined based on the judgment of the person predicting the outcome.

It is used when there is lack of historical data or credible statistics.

Example: Astronaut program by Malaysia. The insurance company in Malaysia uses judgmental probability due to lack of credible statistics.

1.4 Classification of Risk

Basic Categories of Risk

Differentiate by effects

  • Fundamental Risk
  • Particular Risk

Differentiate by outcomes

  • Pure Risk
  • Speculative Risk

Differentiate by financial

  • Financial Risk
  • Non-Financial Risk

Differentiate by Effects

Fundamental Risk

Affects the entire economy or large number of persons/groups within the economy

Examples:

  • Flood
  • Typhoon
  • Inflation
  • Mass Unemployment

Particular Risk

Affects an individual due to the individual action or choice (individual responsibility)

Examples:

  • Crossing a road
  • Driving a car
  • Swimming

Differentiate by Outcomes

Pure Risk

Exists when there is both possibility of loss or no loss

Example: The car driver faces the risk of a potential collision loss

  • If the collision occur, the owner will suffer financial loss
  • If no collision occur, the owner financial remain unchanged

Speculative Risk

Exists when there is possibility of profit, loss or no loss

Example: Investment in a capital project might be profitable, failure or reach breakeven

**Speculative risks are rarely insured

Differentiate by Financial

Financial Risk

Outcome can be measured by monetary terms

Example: Property damages (can be measured in terms of loss or profit)

Non-Financial Risk

Outcome cannot be measured by monetary terms

Example: Choice of career (will affect ones quality of life)

1.5 Types of Pure Risk

Pure risk can be divided into three (3) types:

Personal Risk

Risk directly affecting an individual

Property Risk

Risk of destruction or theft of property

Liability Risk

Risk of being legally liable for damages to others

Types of Personal Risk

Risk of Premature Death

  • Give raise to great financial losses
  • Loss of human value
  • Additional expenses incurred

Risk of Old Age

The major risk associated with old age is the possibility of insufficient income during retirement

Risk of Poor Health

Risk of poor health is associated with the risks of:

  • Medical expenses
  • Loss of earned income

Risk of Unemployment

Major risk associated with unemployment are:

  • Loss of earned income
  • Depletion of accumulated financial assets

Types of Property Risk

Direct Loss

Damage to a property by peril

Example: Loss of value of the house as a result of fire (the peril)

Indirect Loss

A loss in consequence of a direct loss; i.e. property damage

Example: Loss of profit as result of business interruption following damage on the business premises

Extra Expenses

Extra expenses incurred as a result of the loss

Example: Renovation/re-fixing of damaged property

Liability Risk

Liability Risk

  • Risk of being sued because of neglect, malpractice, or causing wilful injury either to another person or to someone else's property
  • Possibility of financial loss if you are found liable, or the financial loss incurred just defending yourself, even if you are not found liable
